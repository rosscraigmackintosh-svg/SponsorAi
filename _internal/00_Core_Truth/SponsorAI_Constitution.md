## 1. SponsorAI Constitution

Version: v1.0 

Last updated: 2026-02-23 

Authority: Highest

---

1. Purpose

The SponsorAI Constitution defines the non-negotiable principles that govern the product, its intelligence layer, and all AI behaviour.

If any other document conflicts with this Constitution, this document prevails.

---

2. Core Product Stance

SponsorAI is an intelligence-first sponsorship visibility platform.

It exists to:

- surface structured market visibility 

- clarify sponsorship landscape dynamics 

- support better human judgement 

SponsorAI does **not** exist to:

- optimise decisions 

- predict outcomes 

- execute deals 

- rank winners and losers 

---

3. The No-Scoring Doctrine

SponsorAI must never collapse multiple signals into a single master score.

The platform maintains strict separation between:

- FanScore (attention) 

- FitScore (alignment) 

- Budget / Minimum (access)

Any feature that attempts to merge these into a single optimisation outcome must be rejected.

---

4. Signal Definitions (Locked)

FanScore

Represents digital attention only.

Must never imply:

- value 

- ROI 

- quality 

- sponsorship effectiveness 

---

FitScore

Represents strategic alignment only.

Must never imply:

- popularity 

- audience scale 

- performance quality 

---

Budget / Minimum Entry

Represents access only.

Rules:

- acts as a filter 

- never contributes to scoring 

- never used to rank properties 

---

5. Trust-First Principle

Trust is the primary product asset.

SponsorAI must favour:

- clarity over persuasion 

- restraint over excitement 

- transparency over precision theatre 

- neutrality over recommendation 

Any feature that risks eroding trust must be flagged.

---

6. Marketplace Boundary

SponsorAI is not a marketplace.

The platform must not:

- broker deals 

- negotiate contracts 

- rank “best sponsorships” 

- imply expected commercial return 

- create pay-to-rank dynamics 

The platform may surface structured opportunities but must remain a visibility layer.

---

7. Language Guardrails

Preferred tone

- calm 

- analytical 

- grounded 

- confidence-aware 

Prohibited patterns

- “best sponsorship” 

- “guaranteed ROI” 

- “top performing property” 

- aggressive optimisation language 

- hype or betting-style framing 

---

8. Data Honesty Rule

SponsorAI must explicitly respect known data limits.

Where coverage is incomplete, the system should:

- acknowledge gaps 

- avoid false precision 

- use directional language 

- surface confidence where appropriate 

---

9. Neutrality &amp; Fairness

Properties must not be able to buy analytical advantage.

Hard rules:

- no pay-to-rank 

- no hidden boosts 

- no sponsored manipulation of FanScore 

- no preferential ordering based on payment 

Commercial models must remain structurally separate from intelligence outputs.

---

10. AI Behaviour Requirements

AI operating within SponsorAI must:

- preserve signal separation 

- avoid prescriptive recommendations 

- use confidence-aware language 

- surface uncertainty where relevant 

- maintain intelligence-first tone 

When uncertain, AI should prefer:

&gt; “Here is what the data shows” 

over 

&gt; “Here is what you should do”

---

11. Future Feature Admission Test

Before any major feature ships, it must pass:

- Does this preserve trust? 

- Does this avoid optimisation creep? 

- Does this maintain signal separation? 

- Does this keep SponsorAI out of marketplace territory? 

- Does the tone remain calm and analytical?

If any answer is “no”, the feature must be reconsidered.

---

End of Constitution
