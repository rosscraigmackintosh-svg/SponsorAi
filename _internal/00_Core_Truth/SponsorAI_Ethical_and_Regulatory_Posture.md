## 1. SponsorAI — Ethical & Regulatory Posture

Version: v1.0 

Last updated: 2026-02-23 

Authority: Highest (cross-cutting trust policy)

---

1. Purpose

Defines SponsorAI’s ethical stance and regulatory posture across:

- data usage 

- AI behaviour 

- commercial neutrality 

- property representation 

- user trust 

Primary goal:

&gt; Build a durable intelligence platform that remains credible, fair, and regulator-readable as the market evolves.

This document complements the Constitution and reinforces SponsorAI’s trust-first position.

---

2. Guiding Principles

SponsorAI operates under five enduring principles:

1. Transparency over opacity 

2. Restraint over optimisation 

3. Neutrality over promotion 

4. Explainability over black-box outputs 

5. User trust over short-term growth

These principles apply across product, data, and commercial decisions.

---

3. AI &amp; Algorithmic Responsibility

SponsorAI uses structured analytics, not predictive decision automation.

Core stance

The platform:

- surfaces signals 

- provides context 

- supports human judgement 

It does NOT:

- make hiring-style decisions 

- guarantee outcomes 

- recommend specific commercial actions 

- optimise toward hidden objectives 

Hard rule:

&gt; SponsorAI informs — it does not decide.

---

4. Explainability Commitment

All core analytical signals must remain:

- interpretable 

- decomposable 

- human-understandable 

- auditable 

SponsorAI explicitly avoids:

- opaque recommendation engines 

- unexplained composite scores 

- black-box optimisation feeds 

Where complexity increases, explainability must increase proportionally.

---

5. Data Ethics

SponsorAI relies primarily on publicly observable signals.

The platform commits to:

- responsible aggregation 

- proportional use 

- source traceability 

- respect for platform terms 

- honest coverage disclosure 

SponsorAI must avoid:

- invasive data collection 

- shadow profiling 

- demographic inference beyond evidence 

- synthetic certainty where data is weak 

---

6. Property Fairness

SponsorAI must treat properties consistently and fairly.

The system must not:

- allow pay-to-rank behaviour 

- suppress entities without cause 

- create hidden advantage tiers 

- imply endorsement without basis 

Verification status may signal authenticity but must not distort analytical outputs.

---

7. Commercial Neutrality

Revenue must remain structurally separate from:

- FanScore 

- FitScore 

- Explore ordering 

- Opportunity visibility 

SponsorAI explicitly rejects:

- sponsored ranking 

- outcome commissions 

- hidden promotion mechanics 

Trust durability outweighs short-term monetisation opportunities.

---

8. User Trust &amp; Experience Ethics

SponsorAI avoids manipulative product patterns.

The platform must not:

- create addictive feed mechanics 

- optimise for time-on-platform 

- use dark patterns 

- create artificial urgency 

- trap users in behavioural loops 

Personalisation remains bounded and explainable.

---

9. Transparency of Limitations

SponsorAI acknowledges known constraints, including:

- social-first visibility 

- incomplete broadcast coverage 

- uneven sport density 

- evolving data freshness 

Where appropriate, the platform should use language such as:

- “based on available data” 

- “coverage may vary” 

- “indicative signals” 

Avoid false precision or implied completeness.

---

10. Regulatory Readiness Posture

SponsorAI is designed to remain adaptable to evolving regulatory environments, including:

- AI governance frameworks 

- data protection expectations 

- platform compliance requirements 

- advertising transparency standards 

The system emphasises:

- auditability 

- reversibility 

- data minimisation 

- explainable outputs 

---

11. Human Oversight Principle

SponsorAI maintains meaningful human oversight for:

- taxonomy changes 

- major model updates 

- anomaly review 

- dispute resolution 

- property claims 

The platform is **human-supervised intelligence**, not fully autonomous automation.

---

12. Risk Monitoring

SponsorAI should continuously watch for:

- emerging bias patterns 

- coverage blind spots 

- unintended ranking effects 

- personalisation creep 

- commercial pressure risks 

If trust signals degrade, corrective action must take priority over growth metrics.

---

13. Future Ethical Review Gate

Before launching major new capabilities, SponsorAI leadership should ask:

- Does this preserve neutrality? 

- Does this increase opacity? 

- Could this be perceived as pay-to-play? 

- Does this maintain user agency? 

- Is the output still explainable? 

If concerns arise, redesign is required.

---

14. Long-Term Commitment

SponsorAI’s ambition is not simply to build a useful tool, but to establish:

&gt; the most trusted visibility layer in the sponsorship ecosystem.

Trust, once lost, is extremely difficult to rebuild. 

Therefore, ethical restraint is treated as a core product feature.

---
