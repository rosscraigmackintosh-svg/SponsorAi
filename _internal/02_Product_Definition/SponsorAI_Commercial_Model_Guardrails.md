## 1. SponsorAI — Commercial Model Guardrails

Version: v1.0 

Last updated: 2026-02-23 

Authority: High (trust &amp; revenue integrity)

---

1. Purpose

Defines how SponsorAI generates revenue while preserving analytical neutrality and user trust.

Primary goal:

&gt; Monetise the platform without allowing commercial incentives to distort visibility, scoring, or discovery.

This document is a structural safeguard against pay-to-rank dynamics and marketplace drift.

---

2. Core Principle

SponsorAI sells **access to clarity**, not **influence over outcomes**.

Users may pay for:

- deeper insight 

- workflow efficiency 

- advanced tooling 

- expanded visibility into the market 

Users must never be able to pay for:

- ranking advantage 

- FanScore influence 

- FitScore influence 

- Explore placement 

- opportunity prioritisation 

Hard rule:

&gt; Revenue must remain structurally separate from analytical truth.

---

3. Acceptable Revenue Streams

The following monetisation patterns are structurally aligned.

3.1 Subscription Access

Paid tiers may unlock:

- advanced filtering 

- expanded comparison capacity 

- portfolio tools 

- deeper historical views 

- export capabilities 

- advanced opportunity views 

These improve **user capability**, not **property ranking**.

---

3.2 Premium Intelligence Features (Future)

May include:

- deeper trend analysis 

- scenario modelling 

- expanded coverage views 

- advanced search capabilities 

Must remain:

- descriptive 

- non-prescriptive 

- non-optimising 

---

3.3 Property-Side Subscriptions

Properties may pay for:

- enhanced profile presentation 

- additional opportunity slots 

- richer media presentation 

- verification workflows 

They must NOT pay for:

- FanScore enhancement 

- FitScore enhancement 

- Explore promotion 

- ranking boosts 

---

3.4 Data &amp; API Access (Future)

SponsorAI may monetise:

- structured data access 

- API usage 

- enterprise integrations 

Provided that:

- core analytical integrity remains unchanged 

- data access does not create hidden advantage tiers 

---

4. Prohibited Commercial Patterns

SponsorAI must not introduce:

- pay-to-rank listings 

- sponsored leaderboard positions 

- boosted discovery placement 

- hidden promotion mechanics 

- outcome-based success fees 

- commission-driven deal routing 

These directly violate the Constitution.

---

5. Sponsored Content Boundary

If sponsored content ever exists, it must be:

- clearly labelled 

- visually distinct 

- excluded from analytical surfaces 

- excluded from FanScore influence 

Sponsored material must never blend into the intelligence layer.

---

6. Affiliate &amp; Commerce Layer (Guarded)

Where affiliate links or commerce pathways exist:

They must be:

- clearly identified 

- structurally separate 

- optional to the user 

- non-influential to ranking 

SponsorAI remains an **intelligence platform first**, not a commerce funnel.

---

7. Pricing Fairness Principle

Pricing should feel:

- transparent 

- proportional 

- capability-based 

- non-coercive 

Avoid:

- artificial feature gating that harms trust 

- paywalls around core truth 

- manipulation through scarcity tactics 

---

8. Internal Separation Requirements

SponsorAI architecture should maintain clear boundaries between:

- analytics engine 

- ranking logic 

- commercial systems 

- billing systems 

Purpose:

&gt; Prevent accidental commercial contamination of analytical outputs.

---

9. Future Monetisation Review Gate

Before launching any new revenue stream, require a structured review:

Must answer

- Does this influence ranking? 

- Does this distort visibility? 

- Could this be perceived as pay-to-play? 

- Does this pressure user decisions? 

- Does this preserve intelligence-first positioning? 

If risk is identified → redesign required.

---

10. Success Criteria

A healthy commercial model should produce:

- strong subscription retention 

- perceived analytical trust 

- clear value exchange 

- sustainable unit economics 

It must NOT produce:

- ranking scepticism 

- marketplace confusion 

- neutrality concerns 

- user distrust 

---

End of Document
