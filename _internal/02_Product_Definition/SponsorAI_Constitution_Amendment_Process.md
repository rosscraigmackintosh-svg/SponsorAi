## 1. SponsorAI_Constitution_Amendment_Process.md

Status: Governance Control Document 

Purpose: Define how constitutional changes are proposed, evaluated, approved, and recorded 

Applies to: Founder, Product, Sales, Enterprise, Legal 

---

I. Why This Exists

SponsorAI’s Constitution protects neutrality, explainability, and institutional trust.

However, markets evolve. Data evolves. Product maturity evolves.

This document ensures that:

- Necessary change is possible

- Governance remains intact

- No silent drift occurs

- All amendments are explicit and auditable

Amendment is allowed. 

Drift is not.

---

II. What Counts as a Constitutional Amendment

An amendment is required if a proposal affects any of the following:

- Ranking behaviour

- Visibility logic

- Paid influence restrictions

- FanScore / FitScore structural separation

- Suppression rules

- Confidence thresholds (band logic)

- Integrity penalty structure

- API exposure limitations

- Export disclosure requirements

- Neutrality doctrine

If unsure, default to escalation.

---

III. Amendment Categories

1. Clarification Amendment

Definition:

Clarifies wording without changing functional behaviour.

Example:

Rephrasing neutrality language for legal precision.

Approval Required:

Founder + Model Owner

Public Disclosure:

Internal documentation only.

Version Impact:

Minor Constitution version bump.

---

2. Structural Amendment

Definition:

Changes how a model, rule, or governance mechanism operates.

Example:

Allowing contextual ranking within defined bounds.

Approval Required:

Founder 

Model Owner 

Legal review (if enterprise tier impacted)

Public Disclosure:

Mandatory.

Version Impact:

Major Constitution version bump.

---

3. Commercial Boundary Amendment

Definition:

Alters any rule related to paid influence, ranking exposure, or neutrality.

Example:

Allowing paid featured placements (currently prohibited).

Approval Required:

Founder 

Full governance group 

Formal written justification 

Drift risk assessment 

Public Disclosure:

Mandatory and explicit.

Version Impact:

Major Constitution version bump.

Additional Rule:

Commercial amendments require 30-day reflection period before activation.

---

IV. Amendment Proposal Process

Step 1 – Proposal Submission 

Proposal must include:

- Description of change 

- Rationale 

- Risk analysis 

- Drift assessment 

- Enterprise impact 

- Legal implications 

- Revenue implications 

Step 2 – Governance Review 

Review conducted by:

- Founder 

- Model Owner 

- Product Lead 

- Legal (if required)

Step 3 – Decision 

Possible outcomes:

- Approved 

- Approved with constraints 

- Rejected 

- Deferred 

Step 4 – Documentation 

All decisions logged in Constitution Register.

---

V. Drift Risk Assessment Framework

Every amendment must answer:

1. Does this introduce implied ranking?

2. Does this introduce commercial influence?

3. Does this reduce transparency?

4. Does this weaken suppression?

5. Does this increase reconstruction risk?

6. Would this survive a regulator screenshot test?

If ≥2 risks triggered → heightened review required.

---

VI. Constitutional Versioning

Versioning format:

Constitution v1.0 

Constitution v1.1 (Clarification) 

Constitution v2.0 (Structural Change)

Each version must include:

- Effective date 

- Summary of changes 

- Rationale 

- Impact statement 

Historical versions must remain accessible.

---

VII. Emergency Amendment Clause

In rare cases (e.g., platform API shutdown, legal mandate):

- Temporary rule modification permitted

- Must be documented within 48 hours

- Must include expiry date

- Must undergo full review within 30 days

No permanent emergency changes allowed without formal process.

---

VIII. Public Transparency Requirement

For any structural or commercial amendment:

SponsorAI must publish:

- What changed

- Why it changed

- What it does not change

- How it preserves neutrality

Trust increases when change is visible.

---

IX. Refusal to Amend

If amendment rejected:

- Rationale documented

- Governance register updated

- No silent reconsideration without new proposal

---

X. Founder Lock Principle

Certain clauses are Founder-locked:

- No pay-to-rank

- No sponsored placement

- No global leaderboard

- No hidden score manipulation

These may only be amended by:

- Founder + formal constitutional rewrite

- Explicit public disclosure

If these clauses are removed, SponsorAI ceases to be SponsorAI.

---

XI. Governance Register

A permanent log must be maintained including:

- Amendment ID 

- Category 

- Date proposed 

- Decision 

- Version impact 

- Drift assessment outcome 

- Implementation date 

Immutable and auditable.

---

XII. Long-Term Principle

SponsorAI must evolve deliberately, not reactively.

If a change increases revenue but reduces structural trust, it must be rejected.

Trust is the long-term asset.

Revenue is a derivative outcome.

---

End of Constitution Amendment Process.
