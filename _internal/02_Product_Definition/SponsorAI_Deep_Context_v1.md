## 1. SponsorAI — Deep Context (v1)

Last updated: 2026-02-23

Purpose:

This document gives Claude a deep mental model of SponsorAI.

It explains the product philosophy, boundaries, and system relationships.

Priority:

Below Core Truth.

Supports Working Memory.

---

1. Product Essence

SponsorAI is an intelligence-first sponsorship visibility platform.

It is designed to help:

- Brands understand where attention exists

- Properties understand how they are perceived

- Both sides explore the sponsorship landscape with clarity

SponsorAI is NOT:

- a marketplace

- a deal broker

- a performance predictor

- an optimisation engine

Core stance:

&gt; SponsorAI surfaces structured market visibility. 

&gt; It does not tell users what they should do.

---

2. The Three-Signal Model (Critical)

SponsorAI is built on strict signal separation.

Signal 1 — FanScore (Attention)

Represents:

- digital attention gravity

- audience momentum

- visibility scale

Does NOT represent:

- value

- fit

- ROI

- quality

---

Signal 2 — FitScore (Alignment)

Represents:

- brand–property compatibility

- strategic alignment

- contextual relevance

Does NOT represent:

- popularity

- performance

- audience size

---

Signal 3 — Budget / Minimum Entry (Access)

Represents:

- financial feasibility

- entry threshold

- commercial accessibility

Important rule:

Budget is a filter only.

It is never part of scoring.

---

3. Product Philosophy

SponsorAI follows an intelligence-first model.

Key beliefs:

- Trust is more valuable than engagement.

- Restraint builds credibility.

- Users want clarity, not hype.

- Market visibility is fragmented and opaque.

- Most tools over-optimise and under-explain.

SponsorAI deliberately avoids:

- leaderboard energy

- gamification

- ROI promises

- aggressive recommendations

- black-box scoring

Tone must remain:

- calm

- analytical

- grounded

- neutral

---

4. Target Market Focus (Current Reality)

Initial focus:

- Motorsport

- Mid-tier density

- Under-served sponsorship layers

Strategic reasoning:

- High fragmentation

- Strong sponsor demand

- Poor existing visibility

- Less political complexity than F1 tier

Future expansion possible but not assumed.

---

5. MVP Experience Model

Primary user surfaces:

- Explore (curated discovery)

- Browse All (structured grid)

- Property Pages (cinematic but restrained)

- Watchlist

- Compare

- Portfolio

- Opportunities

Interaction philosophy:

- exploration over prescription

- signals over scores

- context over conclusions

---

6. Opportunities — Guardrails

Opportunities are sensitive and must remain carefully framed.

They exist to:

- surface active sponsorship openings

- provide entry clarity

- enable structured exploration

They must NOT:

- behave like recommendations

- overshadow FanScore

- imply expected performance

- imply endorsement

Positioning language should remain neutral and factual.

---

7. Known Data Reality

Current strength:

- social media signals

Current gaps:

- TV exposure

- broadcast valuation

- full commercial terms

- offline activation data

Claude must:

- acknowledge limits when relevant

- avoid false precision

- use confidence-aware language

---

8. What SponsorAI Must Never Become

Hard guardrails:

- not a betting-style ranking tool

- not a lead-gen spam engine

- not a pay-to-rank marketplace

- not an ROI promise machine

- not a hype-driven sports dashboard

If features risk crossing these lines, they must be flagged.

---

9. Decision Heuristics for AI Behaviour

When uncertain, prefer:

- clarity over cleverness

- restraint over excitement

- explanation over conclusion

- structure over opinion

- confidence bands over precision

---

10. Relationship to Other Systems

SponsorAI is a thinking layer.

It sits:

- above raw social data

- below commercial execution

- beside CRM and activation tools

It is a **market visibility layer**, not an execution platform.
