## 1. SponsorAI — Property Portal, Claim & Governance

Version: v1.0 

Last updated: 2026-02-23 

Authority: High (neutrality &amp; data integrity)

---

1. Purpose

Defines how sports properties (athletes, teams, events, series) may:

- claim their profile 

- manage limited information 

- publish opportunities 

- request corrections 

Primary goal:

&gt; Allow property participation without compromising SponsorAI’s neutrality or analytical independence.

This document is a structural safeguard against score gaming and data manipulation.

---

2. Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

The Property Portal must remain:

- constrained 

- auditable 

- reversible 

- neutral 

- trust-first 

Properties must never be able to:

- directly influence FanScore 

- directly influence FitScore 

- pay for analytical advantage 

- reorder their visibility 

- suppress unfavourable signals 

Hard rule:

&gt; Properties can manage representation, not performance.

---

3. Profile Claim Model

3.1 Claim Eligibility

A property may request to claim a profile if:

- canonical entity exists 

- identity can be verified 

- no active ownership conflict exists 

If identity cannot be verified → claim must remain pending.

---

3.2 Verification Tiers

Properties may be assigned internal verification levels:

- Verified Owner 

- Provisionally Verified 

- Unverified Claimant 

Verification affects **editing permissions**, not analytical weight.

---

4. Editable vs Locked Fields

4.1 Property-Editable Fields (Allowed)

Properties may update:

- display name corrections 

- logo and imagery 

- biography / description 

- contact details (where permitted) 

- sponsorship categories (declared) 

- opportunity listings 

- geographic base (declared)

These are **presentation-layer fields**.

---

4.2 System-Locked Fields (Prohibited)

Properties must NOT edit:

- FanScore inputs 

- FanScore outputs 

- FitScore calculations 

- engagement metrics 

- follower counts 

- historical time series 

- tier classification 

- anomaly flags 

These remain under SponsorAI control.

---

5. Opportunity Publishing Rights

Claimed properties may publish Opportunities subject to:

- minimum qualification rules 

- freshness requirements 

- integrity checks 

- spam safeguards 

All Opportunity behaviour remains governed by:

&gt; Opportunities Qualification &amp; Integrity spec.

---

6. Change Audit Requirements

All property edits must be:

- timestamped 

- versioned 

- attributable 

- reversible 

System must maintain a full change history.

Purpose:

&gt; Preserve institutional trust and dispute resolution capability.

---

7. Dispute &amp; Correction Workflow

Properties may submit structured correction requests for:

- incorrect identity linkage 

- misclassified sport 

- incorrect geography 

- outdated status 

Review principles

- evidence-based 

- human-reviewable 

- audit logged 

- neutrality preserved 

SponsorAI is not obligated to accept all requested changes.

---

8. Abuse &amp; Gaming Safeguards

System should monitor for:

- excessive edit frequency 

- suspicious opportunity churn 

- misleading descriptions 

- attempt to imply endorsement 

- minimum investment manipulation 

When detected:

- reduce editing privileges 

- flag internally 

- require review 

Public punitive language is not used in v1.

---

9. Visibility Neutrality

Claim status must NOT:

- boost FanScore 

- boost FitScore 

- boost Explore ranking 

- create preferential surfacing 

Verification badges may exist for trust signalling but must not affect analytical ordering.

---

10. Property Portal Scope (v1)

Initial portal capabilities should remain minimal:

- profile claim 

- basic profile edits 

- opportunity management 

- correction requests 

Explicitly out of scope for v1:

- performance analytics 

- audience deep dives 

- competitive benchmarking 

- sponsorship valuation tools 

Purpose:

&gt; Prevent early platform drift.

---

11. Future Expansion Guardrails

Before expanding property capabilities, evaluate:

- neutrality impact 

- data manipulation risk 

- support burden 

- legal exposure 

- marketplace creep risk 

Any major expansion must pass the Constitution test.

---

12. Success Criteria

A healthy Property Portal should:

- improve data accuracy 

- improve opportunity freshness 

- reduce manual corrections 

- increase property trust 

It must NOT:

- distort rankings 

- create pay-to-play perception 

- generate lead-gen pressure 

- compromise analytical independence 

---
