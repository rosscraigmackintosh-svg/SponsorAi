## 1. SponsorAI — Visual System Diagram Specification

Version: v1.0 

Last updated: 2026-02-23 

Authority: High (visual architecture reference)

---

1. Purpose

Defines the canonical visual system diagram for SponsorAI.

This diagram is used for:

- investor communication 

- technical alignment 

- new team onboarding 

- architecture discussions 

- strategic clarity 

Primary goal:

&gt; Make SponsorAI understandable in under 30 seconds.

---

2. Diagram Philosophy

The diagram must feel:

- calm 

- structured 

- layered 

- non-technical in tone 

- intelligence-first 

Avoid:

- overly dense boxes 

- engineering noise 

- microservice spaghetti 

- flashy arrows 

This is a **strategic systems view**, not an infra diagram.

---

3. Core Layout Structure

Use a **left → right flow** with five horizontal bands.

Recommended canvas layout

Top to bottom:

1. Source Data 

2. Ingestion &amp; Identity 

3. Intelligence Engine 

4. Experience Surfaces 

5. Governance Layer (overlay)

---

4. Layer 1 — Source Data (Left)

Label

**External Signals**

Boxes

- Instagram 

- X / Twitter 

- YouTube 

- TikTok 

- Property-declared inputs (small box)

Styling

- light neutral cards 

- subtle borders 

- no brand colours dominating 

Optional caption:

&gt; Public attention signals

---

5. Layer 2 — Ingestion &amp; Identity

Section title

**Data Pipeline**

Boxes

- Ingestion Pipeline 

- Property Graph 

- Normalisation Layer 

Arrow flow

External Signals → Ingestion → Property Graph → Normalisation

Visual tone

This layer should feel:

- mechanical 

- reliable 

- quiet 

---

6. Layer 3 — Intelligence Engine (Center Highlight)

This is the **visual heart** of the diagram.

Section title

**SponsorAI Intelligence Layer**

Two primary nodes (side by side)

LEFT:

FanScore 

Caption: Attention

RIGHT:

FitScore 

Caption: Alignment

---

Important visual rule

These two boxes must be:

- visually equal weight 

- clearly separate 

- never merged 

- never stacked into one score 

Between them (small text):

&gt; Independent signals

---

Supporting box (below)

Confidence Framework 

Caption: Coverage &amp; Stability

---

7. Layer 4 — Experience Surfaces (Right)

Section title

**Product Experience**

Boxes (row or grid)

- Explore 

- Property Page 

- Watchlist 

- Compare 

- Portfolio 

- Opportunities 

Arrow flow

Intelligence Layer → Experience Surfaces

---

Critical tone rule

This area must feel:

- calm 

- product-led 

- not growth-hacky 

- not recommendation-heavy 

---

8. Layer 5 — Governance Overlay (Full Width Band)

This is the **trust moat visual**.

Place as a horizontal band either:

- across the bottom 

OR 

- as a soft enclosing frame

Label

**Trust &amp; Governance Layer**

Include small items:

- Commercial Guardrails 

- Personalisation Policy 

- Property Controls 

- Ethical Posture 

- Drift Monitoring 

---

Visual treatment

- soft neutral background 

- subtle enclosing feel 

- not alarmist 

- not heavy 

This should communicate:

&gt; The system is intentionally restrained.

---

9. Key Annotations (Must Include)

Somewhere on the canvas include three small callouts:

Callout 1

&gt; FanScore reflects attention, not value

Callout 2

&gt; FitScore reflects alignment, not outcome

Callout 3

&gt; SponsorAI clarifies the landscape — it does not choose the sponsor

These are **philosophy anchors**.

---

10. What NOT to Show

Do NOT include:

- database logos 

- cloud provider icons 

- microservice detail 

- ML hype language 

- ROI claims 

- optimisation language 

- “AI magic” visuals 

This is intentional.

---

11. Visual Style Guidance

Recommended design direction (matches your taste nicely):

- off-white background 

- soft greys 

- thin borders over shadows 

- Inter typography 

- one muted accent (teal or violet) 

- generous spacing 

- calm hierarchy 

Reference vibe:

&gt; Bloomberg × Wise × Notion 

(not Silicon Valley growth hacks)

---

12. Diagram Success Test

The diagram succeeds if a new viewer can answer:

- Where does the data come from? 

- What does SponsorAI actually compute? 

- Why are there two scores? 

- Where does the user interact? 

- Why is this trustworthy? 

If any answer is unclear → simplify.
